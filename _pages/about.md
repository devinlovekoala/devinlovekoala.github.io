I build research-grade RAG systems and study the failure modes that
production pipelines tend to paper over — graph noise, structural
information loss in multimodal ingestion, and the gap between
answer-level evaluation and what actually goes wrong inside the
retrieval graph.

My work sits at the boundary between engineering and research.
I care about systems that run reliably, and I care about
understanding *why* they fail when they do.

Current focus: extending [LightRAG](https://github.com/HKUDS/LightRAG)
with full-pipeline noise governance (NoiseFilter-RAG Stage 2), and
developing a circuit-domain modal processor for
[RAG-Anything](https://github.com/HKUDS/RAG-Anything).
